02 · Definition

What BaZi is — and what it is not

The established English phrase “Four Pillars of Destiny” is widely used, but it can sound more deterministic than responsible practice requires. This guide uses Four Pillars as the default term and treats “of Destiny” as a historical translation rather than a promise that the future is fixed.

A useful description

Structured traditional interpretation

BaZi converts birth information into a Gan-Zhi chart and provides a vocabulary for comparing phases, positions and timing layers. It can support reflection when its assumptions and limits remain visible.

An unsafe description

Objective diagnosis or guaranteed prediction

A chart does not clinically diagnose a person, prove personality traits, determine relationship success or guarantee specific events. Precise arithmetic does not automatically validate the interpretation built on top of it.

BaZi is best approached as a historical and reflective system: structured enough to study, variable enough to require method transparency, and never a substitute for agency or evidence-based professional advice.

03 · Chart anatomy

How eight characters become four pillars

The chart’s basic grammar is called Gan-Zhi — 干支, gānzhī. It combines ten Heavenly Stems with twelve Earthly Branches in a repeating sequence of sixty valid pairs. The system has a long documented history as a way to record years, months, days and traditional two-hour periods.

A BaZi calculator assigns one Gan-Zhi pair to each of four time positions. The result is arranged vertically, with the Heavenly Stem above the Earthly Branch.

Interpretive roles vary

Some schools associate pillars with family roles, social domains or life stages. Those mappings are teaching conventions, not universal technical facts.

04 · The symbols

Heavenly Stems and Earthly Branches

The ten Heavenly Stems — 天干, tiāngān — classify Yin and Yang expressions of the Five Phases. The twelve Earthly Branches — 地支, dìzhī — form a separate twelve-part cycle used for years, months and traditional hours.

The ten Heavenly Stems

The stems are technical tokens inside the traditional model. Natural images such as tree, flame, mountain or rain are later teaching metaphors, not fixed personality labels.

The twelve Earthly Branches

Branches are often introduced through zodiac animals, but the branch and the animal are not the same data field. The animal is a familiar cultural association; the chart uses the branch itself, its phase relationships and hidden-stem associations.
