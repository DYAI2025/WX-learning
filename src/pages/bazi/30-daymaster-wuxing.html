05 · Reference point

The Day Master is a reference point, not a personality test

日主rìzhǔ

The Day Master is the Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar. In mainstream Zi Ping practice, it is the main reference point used to describe how the other stems, branches and phases relate within the chart.

This does not mean that a person “is” only one phase or one stem. “My Day Master is Yang Wood” identifies the chart’s reference stem; it does not prove that the person must have fixed traits.

More developed readings consider season, roots, support, restraint and other relationships. Different schools may evaluate those factors differently.

Better question

How does this tradition use the Day Stem to organize relationships within the chart, and which assumptions does this interpretation depend on?

06 · Internal layers

Hidden stems: why the visible eight characters are not the whole model

Hidden Stems — 藏干, cánggān — are Heavenly Stems conventionally associated with the Earthly Branches. A branch may contain one, two or three hidden stems. Practitioners use them when discussing roots, support and phase distribution.

A simple count of the eight visible characters does not capture every phase association used in traditional analysis. Exact weighting is a method decision. Percentages such as 60/30/10 should not be presented as universal unless the named lineage or software algorithm is disclosed.

07 · Relationships

Wu Xing balance means relationship, not equal percentages

Wu Xing — 五行, wǔxíng — is introduced here as the Five Phases. “Five Elements” remains common in English, but these are not chemical elements. They are categories used to describe processes, relationships and transformation within the traditional model.

Generating sequence · 生 · shēng

Wood → Fire → Earth → Metal → Water → Wood

Controlling sequence · 克 · kè

Wood restrains Earth; Earth restrains Water; Water restrains Fire; Fire restrains Metal; Metal restrains Wood.

Why a phase count is only a starting point

A bar chart can help users orient themselves. It cannot, by itself, establish the strength, usefulness or meaning of a phase. Traditional readings may also consider season, roots, hidden stems, support and restraint, combinations, clashes and transformations.

!

“Missing” does not mean deficient

A phase absent from a simple visible count is not a medical, emotional or character diagnosis. Balance does not mean exactly twenty percent of every phase.
