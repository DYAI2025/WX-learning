08 · Method transparency

Why time, place and calculation rules matter

The quality of a chart begins with transparent input handling. A calculator should preserve enough provenance to explain how it reached the pillars, especially near year, month, day and traditional-hour boundaries.

01

Civil input

Entered date, local clock time, birthplace and whether the time is known with confidence.

02

Historical time

Time-zone database, daylight-saving treatment and handling of ambiguous or nonexistent clock times.

03

Calendar method

Solar-term source, year boundary, month rule, day boundary and Zi-hour convention.

04

Solar-time policy

Whether the tool uses civil time, local mean solar time or apparent solar time, with corrections stated.

05

Algorithm identity

Version number, method name and enough detail to reproduce or audit the result.

06

Uncertainty handling

Unknown birth time should produce an unknown Hour Pillar or comparison range — never a fabricated value.

Solar terms and Lìchūn

The 24 solar terms — 节气, jiéqì — divide the Sun’s apparent annual path into seasonal markers. This explains why BaZi month boundaries do not simply match Gregorian calendar months.

Lìchūn — 立春, “Beginning of Spring” — is used as a Gan-Zhi year-change marker in many fortune-calculation traditions. Official Chinese-calendar practice uses Lunar New Year for the calendar year. These conventions answer different questions and should not be collapsed into one universal rule.

True solar time is a disclosed choice, not automatic proof of correctness

Some BaZi methods correct civil time for longitude and the equation of time; others retain civil or local standard time. Near a two-hour boundary, that choice can change the Hour Pillar. A responsible product states the method, shows the corrected time where relevant and explains when an alternative convention would change the result.

09 · Where to continue

Da Yun and HeHun: two different deep-dive paths

大运

dà yùn · ten-year phase cycles

How the natal chart is read through changing phases

Da Yun adds a sequence of approximately ten-year stem-branch phases to the natal chart. Practitioners compare each phase with the original chart to discuss changing emphasis, support and tension over time.

Direction, start age, solar-term reference and rounding vary by lineage and software. A dedicated guide should name the selected method rather than teach one formula as universal.

合婚

héhūn · chart comparison

How two charts are compared without a relationship verdict

HeHun compares two BaZi charts through several traditional relationships rather than a single zodiac match. A responsible modern presentation uses the comparison as a prompt for reflection on difference, support and friction.

It should never produce an objective compatibility percentage, “soulmate” label or pass/fail judgment. Communication, consent, safety and agency remain outside the chart.

10 · Common errors

Eight misconceptions that flatten the system

“BaZi is just the Chinese zodiac.”

The year animal is only one popular association with one Earthly Branch. A BaZi chart uses four stem-branch pairs and additional relationships.

“My Day Master describes my whole personality.”

The Day Master is a reference point. It does not prove fixed traits.

“A missing phase is automatically bad.”

Absence in a visible count is not a personal, emotional or medical deficiency.

“Balance means equal percentages.”

Season, support, restraint, roots and relationships matter.

“True solar time is the only valid method.”

Calculation conventions differ. Disclosure and reproducibility are the quality requirements.

“Da Yun predicts exactly what will happen.”

Da Yun is a traditional timing framework, not a guarantee of specific events.

“HeHun decides whether two people belong together.”

A chart comparison does not replace personal agency, communication or safety assessment.

“Precise calculation makes interpretation scientific.”

Accurate astronomy improves reproducibility; it does not establish empirical causal validity for downstream interpretations.
