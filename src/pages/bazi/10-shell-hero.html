01 · Orientation

A charting tradition, not a fixed verdict

BaZi is written 八字 and pronounced bāzì. The words mean “Eight Characters.” In a standard chart, the year, month, day and hour of birth are each represented by one Heavenly Stem and one Earthly Branch. Four pairs create the Four Pillars — 四柱, sìzhù — and eight visible characters.

Practitioners use this structure as a traditional language for discussing relationship, balance, seasonal context and change. The system belongs to a broader history of Chinese calendrical and correlative thought. Its calculations can be technically precise while its interpretations remain tradition-based, school-dependent and non-deterministic.
